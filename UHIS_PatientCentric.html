The Core Reframe

Every design decision flows from
the question the system asks

Disease-Centric System asks

"What condition does
this patient have?"

Result: the system sees three separate records — NCD, maternal, child. A woman with hypertension who is also pregnant appears as two cases in two modules. Comorbidities are invisible. Care plans conflict. The person is lost.

Patient-Centric System asks

"Who is this person,
and what do they need
to stay well?"

Result: one longitudinal record. Conditions, visits, medications, family context, and social determinants attached to a single person over time. Care is continuous, not episodic. The system sees the person before the disease.

1 minute. This is the philosophical anchor for everything that follows. The reframe is not cosmetic — it changes the data model, the workflows, and what "success" looks like. Make the contrast visceral: "BRAC's shasthya kormi knows Fatema as a person. Our system currently doesn't."
Act I · The Problem

Four failure modes of a
disease-centric system

🧩

Fragmented patient identity

The same person appears as different records across NCD, maternal, and child modules. Comorbidities are invisible. A hypertensive pregnant woman is managed by two teams with no shared view.

🔕

Silent defaulters

Patients who miss follow-ups disappear without a signal — disease-wise or otherwise. CHWs carry flat lists. No one knows who fell through until the next visit, which may never come.

📋

Cognitive overload on workers

Clinicians navigate multiple modules for one patient. CHWs manually synthesise information across disease programmes. Every interaction requires mental work the system should do instead.

📊

Dashboards that report, not act

Current dashboards count consultations per disease. They don't surface: which patients are at risk today, which follow-ups are overdue, or whether last month's interventions actually worked.

1.5 minutes. These four failures compound each other — a fragmented record causes cognitive overload, which causes missed follow-ups, which the dashboard never surfaces. Frame this as a system design problem, not a people problem. BRAC's workers are excellent; the system makes them work around it.

Act III · AI Design

AI must reduce what
the user has to do,
not add new things to learn.

CONSTRAINT

Every AI feature works offline or degrades gracefully. No server required for clinical safety.

CONSTRAINT

Every AI action has a manual fallback. AI assists; humans decide. Always.

CONSTRAINT

UI/UX follows existing workflows — field workers should feel less burden, not learn new patterns.

CONSTRAINT

Summaries surface what requires action — not comprehensive data dumps that clinicians must parse.

30 seconds. This slide sets the design philosophy for all three apps. The key sentence is: "UI/UX follows existing workflows." ASHAs should open the app and see it working the way they already think about their day. Clinicians should see a patient and immediately have context — not navigate to find it.
🏘️

Community App

ASHA / Shasthya Kormi  ·  On-device AI  ·  Offline-first

AI Triage & Visit Prioritisation

CHW opens the app each morning and sees one ranked list — not six disease-programme lists. AI scores every household member by risk (overdue, danger sign pattern, critical medication missed) and orders visits accordingly. Route-optimised. CHW just follows the list.

Voice → Structured Visit Record

After a household visit, CHW speaks in local language. On-device model converts speech to a structured record — weight, symptom, medication, patient linked. No typing. Works offline. Syncs when connected. Follows the existing verbal reporting habit.

Unified Follow-up Queue

Overdue patients surface in one list across all conditions — not split by NCD, maternal, child health. A mother with hypertension who is also overdue for ANC appears once, with full context. CHW acts once, not three times.

Smart Survey Nudges

AI detects inconsistency during surveys — child reported "eating well" but weight dropped three consecutive visits — and automatically prompts a follow-up question. Catches what a tired CHW might miss without adding cognitive load.

1.5 minutes. The most important point here is "one ranked list." A CHW today might be juggling separate NCD follow-up lists, maternal registers, child nutrition registers. AI collapses this to a single actionable queue — same workflow, radically less cognitive overhead. This is what "UI follows existing workflow" looks like in practice.
🏥

Facility App

Doctor · Nurse · Pharmacist  ·  40–80 patients/day

AI-Assisted Triage

Before the patient enters the room, AI pre-scores urgency from CHW notes, last vitals, and flagged symptoms. Clinician sees a brief "reason to prioritise" — not raw data to parse. High-risk patients surface to top of queue automatically.

Ambient Documentation

Clinician speaks during consultation. AI drafts the SOAP note in the background. Review and approve in 10 seconds. Eliminates 3–4 minutes of typing per patient — at 60 patients a day, that is 3–4 hours returned to care.

Smart Pre-Fill

Returning patients arrive with forms pre-filled from history — conditions, medications, last vitals. Clinician corrects exceptions only. Estimated 60% reduction in data entry for follow-up visits. Follows the existing review-and-confirm mental model.

Unified Patient Summary

One screen: active conditions, current medications, overdue tasks, last CHW visit note, pending referrals. No module-switching. AI summarises the relevant history so the clinician asks the next question — not searches for context.

Prescription Safety + Protocol Nudge

Silent checks for drug interactions, allergy conflicts, dosing errors. National protocol surfaced inline — clinician sees the recommendation in context, not in a separate guidelines app. Flags, never blocks. Clinician always decides.

One-Tap Referral + Closed Loop

AI generates referral letter from consultation context. Referral status tracked until completion — if no return signal in 7 days, CHW is alerted. The system closes the loop, not memory or paper.

2 minutes. The "unified patient summary" and "ambient documentation" are the two features that most directly address cognitive load. Frame the triage point clearly: the clinician should never have to mentally sort urgency — the system should present patients in the order that makes clinical sense. Pre-fill follows an existing "review" habit, which is why adoption is likely to be high.
📱

Patient App

Low-literacy · Vernacular-first · SMS-capable · No app required for basics

Plain-Language Visit Summary

After every facility visit, AI generates a 3-line summary: what was found, medicines to take, when to return. Delivered via SMS or WhatsApp in local language. No app required. Patient understands their own care — not just follows instructions.

Between-Visit Symptom Triage

Conversational AI flow — patient speaks or types how they feel. AI triages: safe to wait, visit facility, or call CHW now. Reduces costly and exhausting unnecessary travel while catching danger signs early.

Vernacular Voice Health Assistant

Patient speaks in their language — "Yeh dawai kab leni hai?" AI responds in audio. No reading or typing needed. Works for patients with shared phones or low digital literacy. Designed for the actual user, not the idealised one.

Adherence Capture via Simple Reply

Medication reminders in local language. Patient's free-text reply ("aaj nahi li") is parsed as a missed dose, logged, and escalated to CHW if critical medication. Meets patients in their own communication habits — SMS, WhatsApp.

1 minute. Key point: "No app required for basics." The most important patient-facing AI features (visit summary, reminders) work over SMS/WhatsApp — the patient doesn't need to download or learn anything. This is what it means to design for the actual user, not the idealised one.
Design Philosophy · UX

Reduce cognitive load.
Follow the workflow, don't fight it.

The hardest part of digital health is not building the feature — it is making the feature invisible. The best system is the one workers stop noticing because it just works.

Principle 1

One view per role

CHW sees a ranked visit list. Clinician sees a unified patient summary. Supervisor sees a population panel. No one should navigate to find the thing they need for their job.

Principle 2

Summarise to action

Every summary ends with "what to do next" — not a data review. A patient profile doesn't list 20 data points; it says "3 things require attention today." AI does the synthesis; the worker makes the decision.

Principle 3

Defaults over decisions

Pre-fill rather than blank forms. Suggested diagnosis rather than empty field. Pre-drafted referral letter rather than blank template. Workers confirm and correct — they don't construct from scratch.

Principle 4

Alerts that matter only

Alert fatigue kills adherence. The system surfaces one category of alert: "something requires your action now." Informational noise is suppressed. Every alert implies a clear next step.

Principle 5

Familiar interaction patterns

CHWs already give verbal reports after home visits — voice capture extends that habit. Clinicians already review before confirming — pre-fill extends that habit. New features map to existing mental models.

Principle 6

Graceful offline fallback

Every AI feature degrades to a usable manual mode when connectivity fails. Rural CHWs cannot depend on network reliability. The system must be trusted before it can be relied upon.

2 minutes. This is the design system behind all AI features. "Summarise to action" is the antidote to the current dashboard problem — dashboards that show data but don't tell you what to do. Principle 3 (defaults over decisions) is the key mechanism behind pre-fill and ambient documentation. Principle 5 is why voice capture and pre-fill are the right first AI features — they extend habits that already exist.

Today's panel: who is high risk, who is overdue, who has a care gap. Surfaced before the day starts — not discovered in the consultation.

Control rates, not visit counts. % hypertensives with BP controlled. % ANC completions. Trends over time. Catchment-level gaps visible at a glance.

If they leave knowing only what happened last month, the dashboard has failed.

Technical Architecture

Built for rural reality —
not urban assumptions

Every architectural decision is constrained by the reality of rural Bangladesh: low-end Android devices, intermittent connectivity, semi-literate users, high stakes.

On-device

Edge AI

Small speech model, offline risk scoring, form pre-fill cache. No server call required. Works in zero connectivity.

WiFi sync

Edge Sync

AI processes queued data when facility WiFi available. Results cached locally. Sync is opportunistic, not blocking.

Server-side

Cloud AI

LLM for ambient documentation, population analytics, complex risk modelling. Used when connected; gracefully absent when not.

Always

Manual Fallback

Every AI feature degrades to a usable manual flow. The system is trusted before it is relied upon. No clinical pathway blocked by AI failure.

Language — non-negotiable

All voice and text AI must support Bengali and relevant regional dialects. AI built on English-primary models that poorly handle local language is not fit for purpose. Indic language support is a prerequisite, not a feature request.

1 minute. The architecture choices here are directly driven by user reality — not engineering convenience. The language point is politically and practically important: a voice assistant that mishears Bengali or switches to English under pressure will be abandoned immediately. This is a make-or-break requirement.
Act IV · The Path

Sequenced for trust,
not big-bang disruption

Each phase delivers value independently. No phase requires the next to be useful. Trust is built before dependency is introduced.

Clinical Intelligence
  • Ambient documentation pilot at facilities
  • Prescription safety layer + protocol nudges

Phase 4 · Months 16–24
Patient Engagement + Population
  • Vernacular voice assistant + SMS adherence capture

1.5 minutes. Phase 1 is deliberately AI-free. This is intentional — the structural change (unified record) delivers value immediately and builds the data foundation that all subsequent AI depends on. The sequencing also builds trust: workers see the system improve their lives before it asks them to rely on AI features they don't yet understand.
Why We Know This Works

NCD360° is the proof of concept.
UHIS is the infrastructure to scale it.

What NCD360° demonstrated

By adding community follow-up, patient-centred monitoring, and care continuity — without AI — BRAC and Medtronic Labs tripled hypertension control rates (31% → 72%) and doubled diabetes control rates (23% → 47%) in Bangladesh.

The patient-centric model works. The question is not whether to shift — it is how to systematise and scale what NCD360° validated at programme level, across all of BRAC's health programming, and into every UHIS interaction.

AI does not create this model. AI removes the barriers to sustaining it at scale — the documentation burden, the follow-up memory load, the triage judgment under time pressure.
